PANDA

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is a bear species native to China, known for its distinctive black and white fur. Pandas are primarily herbivorous, with bamboo making up about 99% of their diet, although they occasionally eat other plants or small animals. They have a unique wrist bone that functions as an opposable thumb, helping them grasp bamboo stalks.

Pandas are solitary animals that live in temperate mountain forests with dense understories of bamboo. They have a slow reproductive rate, with females typically giving birth to one cub every two years. Despite their size, pandas are excellent climbers and can swim well.

Due to habitat destruction and low birth rates, giant pandas were once considered endangered. Thanks to conservation efforts, their status has improved, but they remain vulnerable and protected by law.
